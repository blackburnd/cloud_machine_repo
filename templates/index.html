Hello there,

From understaffed university shops to the corporate banking cube farm to lightweight agile development teams, I continue to appreciate foundational knowledge learned through trial, and experience and effective communication. I thrive avoiding the all too common overconfidence related mistakes by maintining an practice of a lifelong academic who always has something to learn.

Building solutions are easy, building solutions better is rewarding, solutions based performance enhancements are addictive.

Its a terribly exiting time to be skilled in software with new tools coming out often, and new AI agents testing my four letter vocabulary and interpretation of patience I decided as an academic excersize to imerse myself in Google's Cloud platform, CI/CD and co-pilot agents in git, as well as vscode. From a person used to terminal console and powering through with emacs and a pdb this has been a change. I am impressed daily by what is possible by these tools in the right hands, and how much can go wrong so quickly while learning.

Python has always been my primary language for solving solutions as well as paying the bills, and often the process to implement the Correct solution is something other than python.

I am a big fan of virtualization and making life easier tomorrow. Historically my development environment would be a VMWare version whatever the production was running on,and my development environment would be an emacs session in a SSH terminal, almost identical to a production SSH terminal, This was so emergency debugging would be efficient, and was all too common in some shops. This also led me to build a python interepreter analog I would use as an admin on our site that saved countless hours, and was a real fun and helpful project.
